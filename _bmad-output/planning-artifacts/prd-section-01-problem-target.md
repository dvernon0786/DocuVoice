### 1. Problem & Target User

#### 1.1 Core Problem
Medical students preparing for high-stakes exams (NEET-PG, INI-CET, USMLE Step 1/2) face these interconnected pain points:

- **Massive volume of dense PDF material** — Marrow videos/notes, Pre-PG PDFs, DAMS notes, First Aid, Pathoma, UWorld explanations, standard textbooks (Robbins, Harrison excerpts, Gray's Anatomy diagrams) — are scattered across drives, WhatsApp groups, Telegram channels.
- **Time sink in flashcard creation** — Manually making Anki cards from PDFs takes hours per chapter; most students never finish converting their entire material.
- **Offline / low-connectivity reality** — Many students in tier-2/3 cities, hostels, or during travel/ward duties have unreliable internet → cloud tools (ChatGPT, Quizlet, RemNote AI, Marrow app full features, AnKing sync) become unusable or expensive on mobile data.
- **Privacy & data concerns** — Indian students (and global ones) are increasingly wary of uploading sensitive study notes/PDFs to foreign cloud services (OpenAI, Groq, RemNote sync, Quizlet) due to data leaks, terms-of-service changes, or geopolitical risks.
- **Anki friction** — While Anki is gold-standard for spaced repetition, it has steep learning curve (add-ons, FSRS setup, image occlusion, cloze formatting), no built-in PDF-to-card pipeline, and poor mobile-first experience on low-end Android phones common in India.
- **Quality of generated cards** — Existing AI tools (ChatGPT, StudyFetch, Knowt) often hallucinate, make vague cards, or ignore clinical context / differentials / high-yield focus needed for NEET-PG/USMLE.
- **No integrated read-aloud** — Many students use auditory review (listen to cards while commuting or in OPD); current tools lack good offline TTS for medical terms/pronunciation.
- **Fragmented workflow** — Students juggle 5–8 apps/tools (PDF reader, Anki, Notion/Marrow app, Telegram for notes, WhatsApp for group PDFs) → context switching kills focus.

#### 1.2 Target User Profile (Primary Persona)

**Name:** Arjun (23–27 years old)
**Background:**
- MBBS intern / fresh graduate preparing for NEET-PG 2026/INI-CET / USMLE Step 2 CK
- Studying in Bengaluru / tier-2 city college/hospital (e.g., BMCRI, MS Ramaiah, St. John's, or peripheral colleges)
- Uses mid-range Android phone (₹15–25k, 6–8 GB RAM) + basic laptop (often shared/family)
- Daily routine: 8–12 hours study + hospital duties + 1–2 hours commute
- Budget: Limited (prefers free/offline tools; willing to pay ₹500–2000 one-time if value is proven)
- Tech comfort: Comfortable with apps/PDF readers, but not deep Anki customization; frustrated by cloud dependency

**Goals**
- Convert Marrow/Pre-PG/DAMS PDFs into high-yield Anki-style cards quickly
- Study offline during travel, night duties, power cuts
- Retain knowledge long-term with proper spaced repetition (FSRS level)
- Have audio read-aloud for pharmacology names / symptom lists
- Keep all study data 100% private/local (no upload to OpenAI/Groq/RemNote)
- Export to Anki .apkg if needed for desktop sync

**Pain Points (direct quotes from forums/Reddit/Telegram groups 2025–2026)**
- "I have 50 GB PDFs but only made 2000 Anki cards in 6 months — too slow"
- "Anki mobile is laggy on my phone, and no good PDF-to-card tool offline"
- "Don't want to upload Marrow notes to ChatGPT — privacy risk"
- "Need cloze for lists (antibiotics, antihypertensives) but manual is painful"
- "Internet slow in hostel — can't rely on cloud AI for card gen"
- "Want to listen to cards while walking to hospital"

**Secondary Personas**
- **Priya** (USMLE Step 1 aspirant in India) — needs First Aid/Pathoma integration, image occlusion for histo
- **Rahul** (PGY-1 resident) — quick revision during short breaks, offline audio review
